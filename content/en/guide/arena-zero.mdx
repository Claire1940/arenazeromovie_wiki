## The Foundation of Planet Zero

Planet Zero serves as the ultimate neutral ground for the Intergalactic Fight. Unlike other worlds that have their own unique ecosystems and "things," Planet Zero is designed specifically for combat. It is the nexus where every world meets, but it is also a graveyard for those who fail to meet the standard.

The rules are simple but absolute: One champion per world. No more battles after the champion is selected. If the champion loses, the world is gone. This "Planet Dive" consequence ensures that every combatant is fighting with everything they have. Interestingly, Earth has a history here; records indicate a previous entry 66 million years ago, where the "wrong guy" was picked—leading to a catastrophic reset for our planet. In 2026, the mantle has fallen to a new warrior named Leo, whose unconventional approach to the arena is already turning heads.

## Combat Mechanics: Hitboxes and Health

The combat in **arena zero** is notoriously technical. Warriors often complain about "bugged builds" and "awful hitboxes," but these are variables that a true champion must account for. Every strike has a specific frame data profile, and understanding the physical reach of your opponent is paramount.

### Understanding the HP System
Champions typically start with a set amount of Health Points (HP). Damage is calculated based on the force of the impact and the location of the strike. For example, a glancing blow might only result in a -5 HP penalty, whereas a direct hit to a limb can cause dismemberment, significantly altering the champion's ability to fight.

### The Medical System: Healing Slime
Standard medicine does not exist on Planet Zero. Instead, champions rely on "Healing Slime." While the application is unconventional (often involving the guide spitting or applying the slime directly to the wound), the regenerative properties are undeniable.

| Mechanic | Effect | Duration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Healing Slime** | Stops bleeding and restores 2 HP/sec | Until wound closes |
| **Death Timer** | 3-minute window to finish the fight | 180 Seconds |
| **Fatigue** | Reduces movement speed and attack power | Permanent until rest |
| **Ability Unlock** | Special move triggered by a symbol | Instant |

## Survival Strategies for the Arena

To survive the 2026 season of **arena zero**, champions must look past the immediate threat and analyze the environment. The "Death Timer" is a common mechanic where, after a certain duration, the arena becomes increasingly lethal to both combatants. This forces an end to the stalemate and rewards the aggressor.

1.  **Speedrunning the Opener:** Use your initial stamina to scout the opponent's reach and movement patterns.
2.  **Environmental Awareness:** Look for symbols or prompts in the arena that might unlock new abilities.
3.  **Managing Mass:** Larger opponents, such as those from Planet Nid, often have high mass. Once they are knocked down, their "short legs" and weight make it difficult for them to recover quickly.
4.  **Conserving Energy:** Do not waste attacks on an opponent who isn't showing signs of fatigue. Wait for the "Heat" moments where they are vulnerable.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The Death Timer is absolute. If neither champion is victorious within the 3-minute window, the arena may execute a "Double Kill" protocol, resulting in the loss of both represented worlds.

The guides also handle the inventory system, allowing champions to swap items or apply healing between rounds. Building a rapport with your guide is just as important as mastering your weapon, as they are the ones who understand the "strategy of running" when a direct confrontation is suicide.

A: The rules of Planet Zero are enforced by the intergalactic organizers. Refusal to fight is treated as a forfeit, leading to the immediate destruction of the champion's home world.

**Q: Can items be upgraded between battles?**

A: Yes. During the 480-hour rest period, champions can refine their inventory. For example, Leo's sneakers can be tuned for even higher movement speed or specialized "Speedrun" capabilities.

**Q: Who are the "Beautiful Freaks" mentioned by the announcer?**

A: This refers to the intergalactic audience watching the stream across a thousand worlds. The tournament is the primary source of entertainment and political resolution for the galaxy in 2026.
