## The Lore of the Thousand Worlds

The universe of **arena. zero** is built upon a brutal foundation: the Intergalactic Fight. There are a thousand worlds, and each one possesses a unique "thing"—a specific cultural or biological trait that defines its people. However, these worlds do not coexist in peace. Instead, they are funneled into Planet Zero, a neutral ground where the ultimate battles take place.

The rules of the tournament are absolute. Each world is allowed only one champion. If that champion falls in battle, their entire world is "gone"—effectively erased from existence. This high-stakes environment explains the desperation and confusion seen in new champions like Leo. Earth has participated in this tournament before, notably 66 million years ago, where a "wrong guy" was picked, leading to a catastrophic extinction event. In 2026, the mantle has fallen to a seemingly ordinary human, raising the stakes for the entire human race.

## Combat Mechanics: HP, Hitboxes, and Timers

Combat on Planet Zero functions similarly to an Action RPG. Champions have visible health points (HP) and must contend with environmental hazards and enemy abilities. A significant challenge for new warriors is the "janky" nature of the arena itself. As noted by seasoned observers, hitboxes can be inconsistent, meaning a strike that looks like a hit might miss, and vice versa.

The tournament also employs a strict 3-minute timer for major encounters. If a champion cannot secure a victory within this window, the pressure increases exponentially. Furthermore, the game world tracks specific injuries. A lost limb or a deep laceration isn't just visual; it affects the champion's performance until they can access the Planet Zero medical system.

## The Healing System: Understanding Slime Science

Medical technology in **arena. zero** is far removed from Earth's traditional medicine. The primary source of recovery is a sentient "healing slime." While the application process—which involves a companion spitting the slime onto the wound—is off-putting to many, its effectiveness is undeniable.

The slime works by moving over the injury and temporarily stopping the bleeding. It is not a permanent fix but a "stop-gap" measure to keep the champion in the fight. Understanding when to pause for a heal and when to push for damage is the hallmark of a veteran fighter.

1.  **Assess the Wound:** If the injury is a "clean" cut, the slime will bond faster.
2.  **Hold Still:** Movement during the application can cause the slime to displace, wasting the charge.
3.  **Monitor the Movement:** The slime is active as long as it is moving; once it settles, the healing effect plateaus.

## Tournament Structure and Future Matches

Victory in the first round is only the beginning. The **arena. zero** tournament is a marathon, not a sprint. After a successful fight, champions are granted a 480-hour rest and recovery period. This time is intended for:

*   **Physical Healing:** Allowing the slime to fully integrate and repair tissue.
*   **Item Upgrades:** Reviewing the inventory for the next round's "meta."
*   **Ability Training:** Mastering any new symbols or powers awakened during the previous heat.

The next battle is always more difficult than the last, as the pool of champions narrows down to the most elite warriors from across the thousand worlds.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if a champion refuses to fight in arena. zero?**

A: There is no option to refuse. The champion is chosen by their planet's "destiny" or internal selection process. Failure to participate or losing the match results in the immediate destruction of the champion's home planet.

**Q: Are the sneakers really better than the sword?**

A: In the specific context of Leo's first fight, yes. The mobility allowed him to survive long enough to trigger a new ability. However, in later rounds of **arena. zero**, a balance of damage and speed is likely required.

**Q: How does the "Healing Slime" work?**

A: It is a biological agent that reacts to open wounds. It acts as a temporary sealant to stop HP drain. It is often applied by the champion's "bro" or guide, such as Hokei.

**Q: What is the significance of the 480-hour timer?**

A: This is the standard "cooldown" period between heats. It ensures that champions are at peak performance for the entertainment of the "freaks" watching the intergalactic broadcast.
