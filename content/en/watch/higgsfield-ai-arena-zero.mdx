## Character Consistency and Design

One of the biggest hurdles in AI filmmaking is maintaining character consistency across different shots. In **Higgsfield AI Arena Zero**, the directors used Soul Cinema to ensure that characters like Leo, Hoko, and Ziki maintained their visual identity regardless of the environment or lighting.

The character Hoko, a fan-favorite support character, was inspired by the quirky dynamics found in shows like *Happy!*. Creating her required numerous iterations to find the right balance of humor and visual appeal. Interestingly, the team initially struggled with her voice, nearly resorting to manual dubbing until the latest Seedance updates allowed for a more nuanced emotional performance.

## World Building: The Circular Arena Strategy

The setting of **Higgsfield AI Arena Zero** is a massive, gladiator-style stadium. To maintain environmental consistency at such a large scale, the directors employed a clever technical "hack": they designed the arena to be circular. This geometric choice allowed the AI to generate shots from any angle while keeping the background recognizable and consistent.

Scouting locations in the AI era is also significantly faster. For the opening apartment scene, the team generated 60 different architectural options in just 10 minutes. In a traditional production, scouting 60 physical locations would take weeks of travel and logistics.
