## The Foundations of Planet Zero

Planet Zero serves as the central hub for the intergalactic fight. Unlike traditional arenas, this world is designed to host a thousand different cultures, each sending one champion to represent their interests. The rules are simple but absolute: if a world’s champion loses their match, that world is destroyed. This creates a high-pressure atmosphere where every decision in the inventory screen carries the weight of billions of lives.

The tournament structure is managed by entities like Hokei, who act as guides for new combatants. Combatants are given a limited window—often only ten seconds—to select their starting items. This "pick phase" is where many champions fail before the fight even begins.

## Mastering the Arena 0 Combat Loop

In **arena 0**, combat is not just about raw power; it is about exploiting the "meta" of the current build. While many warriors gravitate toward heavy weaponry like swords, the current iteration of the arena heavily favors movement speed. As seen in recent matches, high-tier sneakers can be more effective than a blade if they allow the user to outpace the awkward hitboxes of larger opponents.

## Enemy Archetypes and Boss Mechanics

Every opponent in **arena 0** originates from a different planet, bringing unique biological advantages and disadvantages to the floor. For example, warriors from Planet Nid often possess massive physical bulk but suffer from "short-leg syndrome," making them susceptible to being tripped or stuck once they lose their balance.

### Understanding the Death Timer

One of the most brutal mechanics in the arena is the Death Timer. If a fight lasts longer than three minutes without a decisive blow, the system initiates a countdown that will kill both combatants. This mechanic is designed to entertain the "freaks" watching the broadcast by ensuring that every match ends in blood, regardless of how defensive the fighters play.

1.  **Identify the Enemy Weakness:** Look for physical traits like mass-to-leg-ratio.
2.  **Monitor the Clock:** If the 3-minute mark approaches, you must commit to an all-out attack.
3.  **Use Ability Symbols:** Mid-fight symbols can grant temporary boosts that override standard physics.
4.  **Target Limbs:** In some builds, removing an opponent's arm is more effective than targeting their main HP pool.

## Survival and Recovery

Post-combat recovery is just as important as the fight itself. The medical system in **arena 0** is primitive but effective, utilizing "healing slime" that moves and reacts to the champion's biology. While uncomfortable, this slime is necessary to stop temporary bleeding and prevent permanent HP loss.

Between matches, champions are granted a 480-hour rest period. This time should be spent analyzing the next opponent—often referred to as "Combat Machines"—and refining the item build. The **arena 0** experience is a marathon, not a sprint, and managing your mental state after the trauma of intergalactic combat is vital for long-term survival.

> **Tip:** Do not wipe off the healing slime early. Even if it feels like it's moving under your skin, that sensation indicates the cellular repair is active.

## FAQ

**Q: What happens if I pick the wrong items in the arena 0 inventory screen?**

A: You have to adapt your strategy immediately. If you chose speed items over weapons, your goal changes from direct combat to environmental kills and kiting. There are no "do-overs" in the intergalactic tournament.

**Q: How do I unlock new abilities during a match?**

A: Look for glowing symbols that appear in the arena. These are often triggered by specific combat milestones or by surviving for a certain duration. Touching the symbol grants an immediate power-up or a new skill.

**Q: Is the "Bleed" status permanent in arena 0?**

A: No, but it will drain your HP rapidly if not treated with healing slime. Once applied, the slime stops the bleeding temporarily, allowing you to survive until the end of the round.

**Q: Who is Hokei, and can I trust him?**

A: Hokei is your "bro" for the tournament, acting as a guide and mentor. While he provides essential information, remember that his primary goal is to ensure the tournament continues, so always prioritize your own survival instincts.
