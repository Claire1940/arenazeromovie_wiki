### Essential Combat Stats

To survive the arena, a champion must manage several key factors:

*   **HP (Hit Points):** A numerical representation of health. Taking direct hits from massive opponents like the Ogre can result in significant HP loss (e.g., -5 HP for a glancing blow).
*   **Movement Speed:** Crucial for dodging "one-shot" mechanics from larger, slower enemies.
*   **Status Effects:** The arena features realistic damage consequences, such as bleeding, which must be managed with "healing slime" to stop HP depletion.
*   **Ability Symbols:** New skills unlock during combat, represented by glowing symbols that provide temporary buffs or tactical advantages.

> **💡 Tip:** In the world of Arenaa Zero, movement speed is often more valuable than raw attack power. Avoiding a hit is always more efficient than trying to tank damage from a 7-foot opponent.

This creates a collaborative filmmaking process where the audience helps polish the final product.

## Survival Strategy for Planet Zero

If you were to find yourself in the position of an **arenaa zero** champion, your survival would depend on more than just luck. The series emphasizes that every item has a hidden potential. The sneakers Leo chose, for instance, aren't just footwear; they are a weapon that powers up his ability to "speedrun" a fight.

### Key Tactical Considerations

*   **Environmental Awareness:** The arena often features obstacles like closed doors or architectural hazards. Standing still makes you a "mannequin," an easy target for heavy hitters.
*   **The 3-Minute Timer:** Most matches have a strict time limit. Champions must either defeat their opponent or survive until the clock runs out, though the latter often carries penalties.
*   **Mass vs. Mobility:** Larger enemies like the Ogre have significant mass. Once they are knocked down, their "short legs" and weight make it nearly impossible for them to recover quickly, providing a window for a finishing move.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not underestimate the "healing slime." While it may look disgusting and move on its own, it is the only way to stop the "Planet Dive" status effect, which occurs when a champion's injuries become terminal.
